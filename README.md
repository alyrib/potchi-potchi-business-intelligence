# Potchi Potchi Business Intelligence

> End-to-end Business Intelligence case study for a fictional UK-based designer collectibles retailer.

---

# The Challenge

Potchi Potchi is preparing to launch as a small UK-based online retailer specialising in designer collectibles.

Starting with an initial investment of **£20,000**, the company must make informed decisions about inventory, profitability and growth while operating with limited financial resources.

To support these decisions, this project develops a complete Business Intelligence solution capable of monitoring business performance and identifying the optimal time for international expansion.

## Company Profile

**Potchi Potchi** is a fictional UK-based online retailer specialising in designer collectibles, blind boxes and art toys from leading Asian brands.

The company is designed as a lean e-commerce business that begins operations with an initial investment of **£20,000**, operating from a home-based inventory before gradually expanding into dedicated storage facilities and, ultimately, international markets.

Its long-term vision is to become a trusted destination for collectors across the United Kingdom and Europe, while generating sufficient capital to support future expansion into the Brazilian market.

# Project Overview

This repository documents the complete Business Intelligence lifecycle developed to support Potchi Potchi's strategic and operational decision-making.

Rather than focusing solely on dashboard development, this project simulates how a real Business Intelligence initiative would be conducted inside a company, from market research and business analysis to data modelling, Power BI development and executive reporting.

The objective is to answer one fundamental business question:

> **Can Potchi Potchi become a profitable UK-based designer collectibles retailer and generate sufficient capital to expand into Brazil?**

---

# Business Objectives

The Business Intelligence solution aims to support data-driven decision-making by helping stakeholders answer questions such as:

- Is the business profitable?
- How long will it take to recover the initial investment?
- Which brands and collections generate the highest revenue?
- Which products should be reordered?
- When should the company lease external storage?
- When will the business be financially ready to expand into Brazil?
