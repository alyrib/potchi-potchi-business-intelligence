# 1. Executive Summary

## Objective

This market research evaluates the commercial viability of launching **Potchi Potchi**, a UK-based online retailer specialising in designer collectibles, blind boxes and art toys.

The purpose of this research is to determine whether current market conditions support the launch of the business and whether sustainable growth could generate sufficient capital to support future expansion into Brazil.

This document provides the business context for the **Business Requirements Document (BRD)** and the subsequent Business Intelligence solution.

---

# 2. Industry Overview

## Market Outlook

The UK collectibles market has experienced strong momentum over the past several years, driven by the rapid growth of the **Kidult** segment—consumers aged 12 and above who purchase toys and collectibles for themselves rather than for children.

According to Circana, the UK toy market returned to growth in 2025 after several challenging years, increasing by **6% year over year** and reaching approximately **£3.9 billion** in sales.

Collectibles and the Kidult category were among the primary growth drivers.

Across Europe's largest markets, the trend is even more pronounced. Circana reported that the Kidult segment grew by approximately **15% across the EU7** during 2025, highlighting increasing demand from adult collectors.

## Key Findings

- Adult collectors are becoming the largest customer segment.
- Collectibles continue gaining market share.
- E-commerce plays an increasingly important role.
- Limited-edition products stimulate repeat purchases.
- Community engagement drives customer retention.

# 3. Market Trends

## Trend 1 — Rise of the Kidult Consumer

Adults now represent one of the fastest-growing customer segments within the toy industry.

Unlike traditional toy buyers, Kidults generally:

- Purchase for themselves
- Collect entire product series
- Value premium packaging
- Are less price-sensitive when purchasing limited editions

This trend aligns closely with Potchi Potchi's proposed target audience.

---

## Trend 2 — Blind Box Culture

Blind boxes combine collecting with surprise.

Customers often purchase multiple units to:

- Complete collections
- Obtain Secret editions
- Trade duplicates
- Participate in online communities

This purchasing behaviour increases customer lifetime value.

---

## Trend 3 — Social Media

Platforms such as TikTok and Instagram have become major demand drivers.

Popular content includes:

- Unboxing videos
- Collection showcases
- Room decoration
- Rarity reveals
- Live shopping

The recent popularity of Labubu and other Pop Mart characters illustrates how social media can rapidly accelerate demand.

---

## Trend 4 — Limited Editions

Scarcity creates urgency.

Collectors frequently purchase products immediately after release due to concerns about:

- Limited production runs
- Increasing resale prices
- Products selling out

- Validate product-market fit.
- Minimise fixed costs.

Once inventory reaches a predefined operational threshold (approximately **300 units** or **£15,000** in inventory value), Potchi Potchi will transition to a dedicated self-storage facility based on the pricing and operational model of **Shurgard Self Storage Deptford**.

# 7. Business Implications

Based on the market research conducted, the following strategic recommendations are proposed for Potchi Potchi's launch and future growth.

## 7.1 Proceed with Launch

The UK collectibles market demonstrates favourable conditions for a niche online retailer.

The continued growth of the Kidult segment, increasing popularity of blind boxes and strong engagement across social media platforms indicate sufficient demand to support the launch of Potchi Potchi.

The market opportunity justifies proceeding with the business.

---

## 7.2 Prioritise Lean Operations

Given the initial investment of **£20,000**, maintaining a lean operating model during the first year is essential.

Inventory should initially be stored at the founder's residence, eliminating warehouse rental costs while sales volume remains relatively low.

This strategy reduces financial risk and preserves working capital.

---

## 7.3 Introduce Operational Expansion Gradually

Rather than leasing commercial premises immediately, Potchi Potchi should adopt a phased operational model.

Once inventory levels or monthly order volumes exceed predefined operational thresholds, the business should transition to a self-storage facility to increase storage capacity while maintaining cost efficiency.

The timing of this transition should be monitored using operational KPIs developed within the Business Intelligence dashboard.

---

## 7.4 Focus on Inventory Optimisation

Inventory management will represent one of the company's most critical success factors.

The Business Intelligence solution should continuously monitor:

- Best-selling brands
- Best-selling collections
- Inventory turnover
- Days of inventory remaining
- Reorder recommendations
- Slow-moving products

This information will enable data-driven purchasing decisions and minimise capital tied up in unsold inventory.

---

## 7.5 Build a Collector Community

Customer acquisition should extend beyond traditional e-commerce practices.

Potchi Potchi should invest in:

- Unboxing content
- Product photography
- Collector showcases
- Instagram and TikTok engagement
- Customer loyalty programmes

Building an engaged community is expected to improve customer retention and increase repeat purchases.

---

## 7.6 Delay International Expansion Until Financial Targets Are Achieved

Although Brazil represents an attractive long-term market due to its large collector community, international expansion should only occur after the UK operation demonstrates consistent financial performance.

Recommended expansion criteria include:

- Positive monthly cash flow
- Stable profitability
- Recovery of the initial investment
- Sufficient cash reserves
- Sustainable inventory turnover

These indicators will later be monitored within the Business Intelligence dashboard.

---

## 7.7 Business Intelligence as a Strategic Asset

Rather than functioning solely as a reporting tool, the proposed Power BI solution will serve as a strategic decision-support system.

The dashboard will enable management to monitor financial performance, optimise inventory, evaluate operational milestones and assess long-term business readiness for international expansion.

Data-driven decision-making will become a core competitive advantage for Potchi Potchi.

# 8. Business Hypothesis

## Primary Hypothesis

Potchi Potchi can become a profitable UK-based online retailer specialising in designer collectibles by leveraging the growing Kidult market, maintaining lean operating costs during its early stages and using data-driven inventory management to optimise purchasing decisions.
