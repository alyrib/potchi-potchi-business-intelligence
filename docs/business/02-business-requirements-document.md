# 1. Executive Summary

## Objective

Potchi Potchi is a fictional UK-based online retailer specialising in designer collectibles, blind boxes and art toys imported from leading Asian brands.

The purpose of this project is to design and implement a complete Business Intelligence solution capable of supporting strategic and operational business decisions throughout the company's early growth stage.

The solution will provide management with actionable insights regarding sales performance, profitability, inventory management, customer behaviour and long-term expansion planning.

---

# 2. Business Background

Potchi Potchi will launch as a small home-based online retailer operating in the United Kingdom.

The business starts with an initial investment of **£20,000**, which will be allocated across inventory acquisition, operating expenses, packaging materials, marketing activities and contingency funds.

Initially, all inventory will be stored at the owner's residence.

As sales volume increases, inventory capacity and operational efficiency will become limiting factors. Once predefined business thresholds are reached, the company intends to migrate inventory to a dedicated storage facility.

For the purpose of this project, warehouse expansion will be modelled using the **Shurgard Self Storage – Deptford** facility as a realistic business scenario.

The company's long-term vision is to become a recognised retailer within the UK collectibles market before expanding into Europe and eventually entering the Brazilian market.

---

# 3. Business Objectives

The Business Intelligence solution should support the following strategic objectives:

- Monitor overall business performance.
- Maximise business profitability.
- Identify best-selling products and collections.
- Improve inventory planning.
- Minimise overstock and stock shortages.
- Understand customer purchasing behaviour.
- Support pricing decisions.
- Evaluate business growth.
- Determine the optimal timing for warehouse expansion.
- Assess financial readiness for international expansion.

---

# 4. Business Problems

Currently, business decisions would rely primarily on intuition rather than data.

Management lacks visibility into:

- Which products generate the highest revenue.
- Which products generate the highest profit.
- Which collections sell the fastest.
- Seasonal purchasing behaviour.
- Customer purchasing patterns.
- Inventory turnover.
- Cash flow performance.
- Monthly profitability.
- Operational expenses.
- Overall business health.

Without reliable reporting, business growth may become inefficient and financially risky.

The initial scope of this project supports business operations within the United Kingdom only.

Future business expansion into Ireland and other European markets is considered out of scope for the initial implementation but has been taken into account during the solution design.

# 7. Business Requirements

## Sales Analysis

The business must be able to:

- Monitor daily sales.
- Monitor monthly sales.
- Compare sales performance over time.
- Identify best-selling products.
- Measure revenue growth.
- Analyse seasonal demand.

---

## Inventory Analysis

The business must be able to:

- Monitor current inventory.
- Receive low-stock alerts.
- Identify overstocked products.
- Calculate inventory turnover.
- Measure inventory value.

---

## Financial Analysis

The business must be able to:

- Calculate Gross Profit.
- Calculate Net Profit.
- Track operating expenses.
- Measure profit margins.
- Estimate cash flow performance.

---

## Customer Analysis

The business must be able to:

- Track repeat purchases.
- Measure customer retention.
- Calculate Average Order Value.
- Identify purchasing trends.

---

## Business Expansion

Management should be able to determine:

- When home inventory storage reaches operational capacity.
- When renting warehouse space becomes financially viable.
- Whether sufficient profitability exists to support expansion into Brazil.

# 8. Success Metrics (KPIs)

The dashboard will monitor the following Key Performance Indicators:

| KPI | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Revenue | Total sales revenue |
| Orders | Number of completed orders |
| Units Sold | Quantity of products sold |
| Gross Profit | Revenue minus Cost of Goods Sold |
| Net Profit | Final business profit |
| Gross Margin | Gross Profit percentage |
| Net Margin | Net Profit percentage |
| Average Order Value | Average customer spend |
| Inventory Value | Current inventory investment |
| Stock Turnover | Inventory rotation speed |
| Customer Retention Rate | Returning customer percentage |
| Monthly Growth | Revenue growth compared to previous month |

---

# 9. Business Assumptions

This project assumes that:

- Sales transactions are accurately recorded.
- Inventory movements are properly tracked.
- Product costs remain relatively stable.
- Shipping costs are consistently recorded.
- Exchange rates remain reasonably stable.
- Suppliers maintain consistent product availability.

---

# 10. Constraints

Current business limitations include:

- Single-person operation.
- Limited home storage capacity.
- Limited initial investment.
- Limited marketing budget.
- Imported inventory with long lead times.
- Exchange rate volatility.

---

# 11. Risks

Potential business risks include:

- Overstocking products.
- Running out of popular inventory.
- Supplier delays.
- Shipping disruptions.
- Currency fluctuations.
- Lower-than-expected customer demand.
- Seasonal sales declines.

---

# 12. Out of Scope

This project does not include:

- E-commerce website development.
- Customer authentication.
- Payment gateway implementation.
- Warehouse Management System (WMS).
- Supplier Management System.
- Accounting software integration.
- Marketing campaign automation.

These initiatives may become future Business Intelligence projects.
